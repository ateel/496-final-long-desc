Animal Abandonment

It's a bigger problem than you think!

The humane society is a wonderful resource.

It takes in 6-8 million animals annually nationwide. Thousands of people volunteer their time and it fascilitates the adoption of millions of animals every year. However, the humane society simply can't keep up with the demand. There are far too many animals brought to these shelters by owners who no longer want them. The humane society just doesn't have the resources to care for them all. Because of overcrowding, policy allows animals to be euthanized as soon as 48 hours after the arrive at a shelter.

The number of in-need animals has risen 65% in the last 7 years. The number of shelters in the US has decreased to under 5,000.

Animals pay the price for overcrowding

Half of all shelter animals are euthanized due to overcrowding. That means...

  • 4,000,000 die every year
  • 80,000 die every week
  • 5,500 die every day

Only 10% of dogs and 3% of cats ever find a permanent home

20% of pets are abandoned after only 6 months!
